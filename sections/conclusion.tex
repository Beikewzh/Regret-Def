\section{Conclusion}
In this work, we proposed the \textit{agent-examiner} framework and the perceived regret $\rho^\chi$ as an approach to measure the performance of a continual reinforcement learning agent. 
We established a no-free-lunch theorem showing there are no universal examiners capable of accurately evaluating any agent in any world. In light of this, the examiner must make certain (potentially implicit) modeling assumptions about the world. 
Through experiments, we demonstrate how perceived regret can be utilized in practice and succeed at evaluating agents. 
We believe these ideas open the door to many further explorations into the design of examiners and evaluation of continual learning agents. As a flexible approach, examiners can be extended to measure other qualities, such as exploration, and perceived regret can serve as an objective of its own.
