\section{Formal Definitions and Assumptions}
\label{sec:defs}

We work over a single, never-resetting stream under an unknown law $\mu$, with no Markov, episodic, or stationarity assumption. Let $\Filt=\sigma(h_t)$ be the filtration generated by the observed history. We collect the standing assumptions.

\begin{assumption}[Standing assumptions]
\label{ass:all}
\textnormal{(A1)} rewards satisfy $r_s\in[0,\Rmax]$. \textnormal{(A2)} value uses a fixed $H$-step look-ahead. \textnormal{(A3)} the learner is representable in $K_L$ bits, with policy class $\PiK$, $|\PiK|\le 2^{K_L}$. \textnormal{(A4)} the examiner carries an inductive bias, a model class $\ModE$ of laws it deems possible. \textnormal{(A5)} all guarantees hold with probability at least $1-\alpha$ over the stream. \textnormal{(A6)} the learner's policy $\pi_t$ and the examiner's outputs $\Vhi,\Vlow$ are $\Filt$-measurable; the agent acts on its stream alone, at any computational budget.
\end{assumption}

\begin{definition}[Stream, policy, value]
\label{def:basic}
A history is $h_t=(o_0,r_0,a_1,\dots,a_t,o_t,r_t)$. A policy $\pi$ maps histories to action distributions, with value
$$f^{\pi}_t=\Ex_{\mu,\pi}\!\Big[\textstyle\sum_{s=t+1}^{t+H} r_s \;\Big|\; h_t\Big].$$
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Within-capacity optimum and structural gap]
\label{def:capcomp}
The within-capacity optimum is $f^{K_L}_t=\sup_{\pi\in\PiK} f^{\pi}_t$, and the structural gap to the global optimum $f^{*}_t=\sup_{\pi}f^\pi_t$ is $\Dstruct_t=f^{*}_t-f^{K_L}_t\ge 0$. We compare the agent to $f^{K_L}_t$ and keep $\Dstruct_t$ explicit as the irreducible price of bounded capacity.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Learner and examiner]
\label{def:agent}
An agent is a pair $(\mathcal{L},\mathcal{E})$. The learner $\mathcal{L}$ emits $\pi_t\in\PiK$. The examiner $\mathcal{E}$ is an $\Filt$-measurable predictive model outputting $\Vhi\ge\Vlow$. Since $f^{K_L}_t$ depends on policies the learner may not run, estimating $\Vhi$ requires predicting the value of unexecuted behaviour, in the sense of general value functions \citep{sutton2011horde,modayil2014nexting} and off-policy prediction \citep{precup2001offpolicy,suttonprecupsingh1999}, with $\ModE$ the bias enabling generalization beyond the support of the data \citep{osband2013psrl}.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Examiner regret / perceived regret]
\label{def:exreg}
$\rho_t=\Vhi-\Vlow\ge 0$, with cumulative form $\Rex_T=\sum_{t=1}^T\rho_t$. As an $\Filt$-measurable functional, $\rho_t$ never refers to $\mu$, $f^{K_L}_t$, or $f^{*}_t$.
\end{definition}

\subsection{Soundness: the examiner must be learning}
\label{sec:examiner-learns}

By itself, $\rho_t$ is just a self-report. What turns it into a certificate is a condition tying the two numbers $(\Vhi,\Vlow)$ to value. We state it as an assumption that the examiner is doing its job; Section~\ref{sec:nothing} shows why no certificate can hold without it.

\begin{definition}[Soundness]
\label{def:sound}
The examiner is \emph{sound} with slack $(\delta_t)$ if, with probability at least $1-\alpha$, for all $t$,
\[
\Vlow\le f^{\pi_t}_t \quad\text{(floor / lower bound)} \qquad\text{and}\qquad \Vhi\ge f^{K_L}_t-\delta_t \quad\text{(ceiling / optimism)} .
\]
It is \emph{consistent} if $\tfrac1T\sum_{t\le T}\delta_t\to 0$.
\end{definition}

The floor is a lower-bound assumption on the played policy's value; the ceiling is an optimism assumption on the within-capacity optimum. Together they place $f^{\pi_t}_t$ and $f^{K_L}_t$ inside the interval $[\Vlow,\Vhi+\delta_t]$. We adopt the convention $\delta_t=0$ unless stated otherwise. The condition is operational: each component can be tested against the agent's own stream by the usual concentration arguments.

\begin{lemma}[Certificate / consistency of perceived regret]
\label{lem:bridge}
If the examiner is sound with slack $(\delta_t)$ then, with probability at least $1-\alpha$,
\[
\sum_{t=1}^T\big(f^{K_L}_t-f^{\pi_t}_t\big)\;\le\;\Rex_T+\sum_{t=1}^T\delta_t,
\qquad
\sum_{t=1}^T\big(f^{*}_t-f^{\pi_t}_t\big)\;\le\;\Rex_T+\sum_{t=1}^T\delta_t+\sum_{t=1}^T\Dstruct_t .
\]
In particular, driving $\rho_t\to 0$ drives the true within-capacity regret to zero.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
On the soundness event of Definition~\ref{def:sound}, decompose $f^{K_L}_t-f^{\pi_t}_t=(f^{K_L}_t-\Vhi)+(\Vhi-\Vlow)+(\Vlow-f^{\pi_t}_t)$. The first summand is at most $\delta_t$ by the ceiling, the third is at most $0$ by the floor, and the middle is $\rho_t$. Summing over $t$ gives the first inequality; adding $\sum_t\Dstruct_t=\sum_t(f^{*}_t-f^{K_L}_t)$ gives the second. \textbf{[proved]}
\end{proof}

The identity is the optimism and performance-difference decomposition \citep{kakade2002cpi,jaksch2010ucrl2}; the content is that under soundness the $\Filt$-measurable $\Rex_T$ upper-bounds a quantity, $\sum_t(f^{K_L}_t-f^{\pi_t}_t)$, that is not itself $\Filt$-measurable. Meeting the ceiling demands strictly more than acting: the examiner must lower-bound a supremum over $\PiK$, whereas the learner need only realize one $\pi_t\in\PiK$. This is the formal sense in which the examiner must out-learn the learner -- which we return to in Section~\ref{sec:practice}.

\paragraph{Local notion.}
Replacing $\PiK$ in Definition~\ref{def:capcomp} by a smaller class $\Pi'\subseteq\PiK$ yields a \emph{local} perceived regret w.r.t.\ $\Pi'$. This is useful when a global upper bound is vacuous: an examiner may certify progress against a tractable comparator class even when the full within-capacity optimum is out of reach. The bridge lemma applies verbatim with $\Pi'$ in place of $\PiK$.
