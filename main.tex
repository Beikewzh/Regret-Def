\begin{abstract}
\textbf{[TODO: finalize abstract.]} Regret measures learning against an optimum that, in every standard formulation, is supplied from outside the agent. Continual reinforcement learning under the big-world hypothesis denies the agent this object: the world is larger than the agent can represent, and the best agents never stop learning \citep{abel2023definition}. We propose \emph{examiner regret}, an intrinsic and capacity-constrained measure of learning. The agent is decomposed into a \emph{learner} that selects actions, holding $K_L$ bits, and an \emph{examiner} that forecasts an aspiration $\Vhi_t$ and a guarantee $\Vlow_t$ on the learner's value, holding $K_E$ bits; examiner regret is $\rho_t = \Vhi_t - \Vlow_t$, defined entirely from the agent's internal state. We treat $\rho_t$ as the primary object: we establish its intrinsic properties (Proposition~\ref{prop:intrinsic}), show that a \emph{sound} examiner's $\rho_t$ upper-bounds the agent's regret against the best policy it could itself represent while displaying the irreducible structural gap to the global optimum (Theorem~\ref{thm:bridge}), recover the classical $O(\sqrt{KT\log T})$ bandit rate as a special case, and prove that in a big world no bounded examiner can honestly drive $\rho_t$ to zero (Theorem~\ref{thm:ignorance}). The contribution is conceptual: an intrinsic, capacity-aware notion of regret that frames evaluation in continual RL as a problem of allocating bits between acting and judging.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

A continual-learning agent, in the sense made precise by \citet{abel2023definition}, never stops learning: it does not converge to a fixed policy but keeps adapting. This view is motivated by the \emph{big-world hypothesis} \citep{javed2025bigworld,lewandowski2025bigger}, under which the agent's representational capacity is far smaller than the complexity of its world, so a fixed solution is neither learnable nor retainable. Continual reinforcement learning (CRL) thus describes agents that are permanently and irreducibly bounded relative to their environment.

Our instrument for certifying learning, however, was built for the opposite case. Regret, the cumulative price of not knowing the environment \citep{lai1985asymptotically,jaksch2010ucrl2}, is defined against a comparator the agent is presumed able to match: the optimal value $V^{*}$. Obtaining $V^{*}$ requires a model, a prior, or a fixed comparator class supplied from outside the agent. In a big world none of these is available, and worse, even if $V^{*}$ were known it would be the wrong target: a bounded agent cannot represent the optimal policy, so measuring it against $V^{*}$ confounds what it failed to learn with what it could never have expressed. This second point is the lesson of bounded optimality and resource-rational analysis \citep{russell1995bounded,lewis2014computational,lieder2020resource}: the right benchmark for a bounded agent is the best use of its own resources, not an unattainable ideal.

We therefore change the starting point. Rather than measure the agent against an external optimum, we ask the agent to measure \emph{itself}. We split the agent into a \emph{learner}, which selects actions, and an \emph{examiner}, which forecasts two internal quantities: an \emph{aspiration} $\Vhi_t$ for the best value it believes attainable and a \emph{guarantee} $\Vlow_t$ for the value it is confident it achieves. We call the gap
\[
\rho_t \;=\; \Vhi_t - \Vlow_t
\]
the \emph{examiner regret}. Crucially, $\rho_t$ is defined from the agent's internal state alone; it is a self-assessed learning signal in the lineage of learning-progress and compression-progress accounts of intrinsic motivation \citep{schmidhuber2010formal,oudeyer2007intrinsic}, but expressed in value units and indexed by the agent's capacity. Each module carries its own bit budget, $K_L$ for the learner and $K_E$ for the examiner, with total $K = K_L + K_E$.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
We treat examiner regret as a first-class object, in three steps. (i) We define it intrinsically and establish its basic properties without reference to the environment optimum (Proposition~\ref{prop:intrinsic}). (ii) We then connect it to ground truth on our own terms: when the examiner is \emph{sound}, $\rho_t$ upper-bounds the agent's regret against the best policy it could itself represent, the \emph{capacity-constrained optimum}, with the irreducible gap to the global optimum displayed rather than hidden (Theorem~\ref{thm:bridge}); the stochastic bandit recovers the classical rate (Section~\ref{sec:bandit}). (iii) We prove a separation showing the limit of self-evaluation: in a big world, no bounded examiner can drive $\rho_t$ to zero while remaining sound (Theorem~\ref{thm:ignorance}). We make no claim to a new algorithm or a tight bound beyond prior work; the contribution is a capacity-constrained, intrinsic notion of regret and the allocation question it raises.

\section{Setting and Definitions}
\label{sec:defs}

\paragraph{Setting.} An agent interacts with an unknown environment over a single, never-resetting stream of experience; we make no MDP, POMDP, or stationarity assumption. Rewards are bounded, $r_t \in [0,\Rmax]$, and value is measured over a look-ahead window of $H$ steps. The agent is capacity-bounded: its learner and examiner are each implemented within a fixed number of bits. Our goal is a \emph{definition} of a learning measure computable from the agent's internal state, together with the conditions under which it certifies learning and the limits beyond which it cannot.

\begin{definition}[Stream and history]
\label{def:stream}
A \emph{stream} is a sequence $\{(o_t, r_t, a_t)\}_{t \ge 0}$ of observations $o_t \in \mathcal{O}$, rewards $r_t \in [0,\Rmax]$, and actions $a_t \in \Acts$. The \emph{history} at time $t$ is $h_t := (o_0, r_0, a_1, \dots, a_t, o_t, r_t) \in \Hist_t$. By the Ionescu--Tulcea theorem the conditional kernels extend to a measure $\mu$ on $(\mathcal{O}\times\mathbb{R}\times\Acts)^{\infty}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Policy and value]
\label{def:value}
A \emph{policy} is a map $\pi : \Hist \to \Delta(\Acts)$. Its \emph{value} at $h_t$ is $f^{\pi}_t := \Ex_{\mu, \pi}\big[\sum_{s=t+1}^{t+H} r_s \mid h_t\big]$, over the environment's and the policy's randomness conditional on $h_t$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Capacity-constrained comparator]
\label{def:capcomp}
Let $\PiK := \{\pi : |\Theta_L| \le 2^{K_L} \text{ represents } \pi\}$ be the policies expressible within the learner's $K_L$-bit budget. The \emph{capacity-constrained optimum} is $f^{K_L}_t := \sup_{\pi \in \PiK} f^{\pi}_t$, and the \emph{structural gap} to the global optimum $f^{*}_t := \sup_{\pi} f^{\pi}_t$ is $\Dstruct_t := f^{*}_t - f^{K_L}_t \ge 0$.
\end{definition}

The structural gap is the part of optimality a $K_L$-bit learner can never reach; it is the price of being small in a big world, and we keep it visible throughout rather than absorbing it into the comparator.

\begin{definition}[Learner--examiner agent]
\label{def:agent}
An \emph{agent} is a pair $\mathcal{A} = (\mathcal{L}, \mathcal{E})$. The \emph{learner} $\mathcal{L} : \Hist_t \to \Delta(\Acts)$ emits a policy $\pi_t \in \PiK$, parametrized in $\Theta_L$ with $|\Theta_L| \le 2^{K_L}$. The \emph{examiner} $\mathcal{E} = (U, L)$, with $U, L : \Hist_t \to \mathbb{R}$ and $U \ge L$ pointwise, emits the \emph{aspiration} $\Vhi_t := U(h_t)$ and the \emph{guarantee} $\Vlow_t := L(h_t)$, parametrized in $\Theta_E$ with $|\Theta_E| \le 2^{K_E}$. The \emph{total capacity} is $K := K_L + K_E$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Examiner regret]
\label{def:exreg}
The \emph{examiner regret} at time $t$ is
\[
\rho_t \;:=\; \Vhi_t - \Vlow_t \;\ge\; 0,
\]
with cumulative form $\Rex_T := \sum_{t=1}^{T}\rho_t$. It is a function of the history and the examiner's parameters only, with no reference to $\mu$, $f^{\pi}$, or any optimum.
\end{definition}

Definition~\ref{def:exreg} is the object of the paper. It is well-posed by construction ($U \ge L$ gives $\rho_t \ge 0$) and depends on nothing the agent cannot compute. The remaining definitions are \emph{conditions} that relate this internal object to the environment; they are not part of $\rho_t$.

\begin{definition}[Soundness and consistency]
\label{def:sound}
The examiner is \emph{sound} on $[1,T]$ with slack $(\delta_t)$, $\delta_t \ge 0$, if with probability at least $1-\eta$, for all $t \le T$,
\begin{align}
\text{(S1)}\quad & \Vlow_t \le f^{\pi_t}_t, &
\text{(S2)}\quad & \Vhi_t \ge f^{K_L}_t - \delta_t. \label{eq:sound}
\end{align}
It is \emph{consistent} if in addition (S3) $\Gamma_T := \sum_{t=1}^{T}\delta_t = o(T)$.
\end{definition}

Soundness asks the guarantee to be a true lower bound on the policy's value (S1) and the aspiration to be optimistic for \emph{the best policy the learner could represent} (S2), not for the global optimum. This is the resource-rational choice of comparator \citep{russell1995bounded,lieder2020resource}: a bounded examiner is asked to recognize the best the bounded learner could do, a target within reach, while the structural gap $\Dstruct_t$ is accounted for separately.

\paragraph{The canonical examiner.}
One construction realizes Definition~\ref{def:agent} for any model class. Let $\nu_t \in \Delta(\Models)$ be a posterior over models, updated from $h_t$, and set $\Vhi_t = \Ex_{M\sim\nu_t}[V^{K_L}_M]$ and $\Vlow_t = \Ex_{M\sim\nu_t}[V^{\pi_t}_M]$, where $V^{\pi}_M$ is the value of $\pi$ under model $M$ (Definition~\ref{def:value} with $\mu{=}M$) and $V^{K_L}_M := \sup_{\pi\in\PiK} V^{\pi}_M$. Every examiner in this paper approximates this construction under a particular $\Models$, $K_E$-bit surrogate for $\nu_t$, and confidence rule; it specializes to confidence bounds (UCB), optimistic value iteration (PAC-MDP), and posterior value gaps (BAMDP), so these are computable approximations of one object rather than separate definitions.

\section{Examiner Regret as an Intrinsic Object}
\label{sec:intrinsic}

We first record what $\rho_t$ means on its own, before relating it to the environment.

\begin{proposition}[Intrinsic properties]
\label{prop:intrinsic}
For any examiner of Definition~\ref{def:agent}: \textnormal{(i)} $\rho_t \ge 0$, with $\rho_t = 0$ iff the examiner's aspiration equals its guarantee, i.e.\ it certifies no representable improvement over $\pi_t$; \textnormal{(ii)} $\rho_t$ is invariant to reward translation $r \mapsto r + c$ and scales linearly under $r \mapsto \lambda r$, $\lambda > 0$; \textnormal{(iii)} $\rho_t$ is computable from $h_t$ and $\theta^E_t$ alone.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
(i) $U \ge L$ in Definition~\ref{def:agent} gives $\rho_t \ge 0$, with equality iff $U(h_t) = L(h_t)$; by Definition~\ref{def:exreg} this is the statement that the aspiration and guarantee coincide. (ii) Both $\Vhi_t$ and $\Vlow_t$ are forecasts of an $H$-step sum of rewards, so an affine reward map $r \mapsto \lambda r + c$ sends each to $\lambda(\cdot) + cH$; the additive $cH$ cancels in $\rho_t = \Vhi_t - \Vlow_t$, leaving $\rho_t \mapsto \lambda \rho_t$. (iii) Immediate from $\Vhi_t = U(h_t)$, $\Vlow_t = L(h_t)$ with $U, L$ measurable functions of the history under $\theta^E_t$.
\end{proof}

Property (i) gives the operational reading we want: $\rho_t$ is a self-assessed learning signal, positive while the agent believes a representable improvement remains and zero when it does not, in the spirit of learning-progress accounts \citep{schmidhuber2010formal,oudeyer2007intrinsic} but in value units. Whether $\rho_t = 0$ is \emph{trustworthy}, i.e.\ corresponds to actual optimality, is exactly the question soundness and Section~\ref{sec:limits} address.

\section{When Examiner Regret Certifies Learning}
\label{sec:bridge}

We now link the intrinsic object to the environment, on the resource-rational terms set above.

\begin{theorem}[Soundness bridge]
\label{thm:bridge}
Let $\mathcal{A} = (\mathcal{L},\mathcal{E})$ act on a stream, with $\mathcal{L}$ emitting $\pi_t \in \PiK$, and let $\mathcal{E}$ be sound with slack $(\delta_t)$ on $[1,T]$. Then with probability at least $1-2\eta$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:bridge}
\CapReg_T \;:=\; \sum_{t=1}^{T}\big(f^{K_L}_t - f^{\pi_t}_t\big) \;\le\; \Rex_T + \Gamma_T,
\end{equation}
and consequently the global dynamic regret satisfies
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:global}
\DynReg_T \;:=\; \sum_{t=1}^{T}\big(f^{*}_t - f^{\pi_t}_t\big) \;\le\; \Rex_T + \Gamma_T + \sum_{t=1}^{T}\Dstruct_t,
\end{equation}
where $\sum_t \Dstruct_t$ is the irreducible structural cost of the learner's bounded capacity.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For each $t$, insert and cancel the examiner's outputs:
\[
f^{K_L}_t - f^{\pi_t}_t = \underbrace{\big(f^{K_L}_t - \Vhi_t\big)}_{\le\, \delta_t \text{ by (S2)}} + \underbrace{\big(\Vhi_t - \Vlow_t\big)}_{=\, \rho_t} + \underbrace{\big(\Vlow_t - f^{\pi_t}_t\big)}_{\le\, 0 \text{ by (S1)}} \;\le\; \delta_t + \rho_t.
\]
This holds on the joint event of (S1) and (S2), which has probability at least $1-2\eta$ by the union bound. Summing over $t=1,\dots,T$ gives \eqref{eq:bridge}. For \eqref{eq:global}, add $\sum_t \Dstruct_t = \sum_t (f^{*}_t - f^{K_L}_t)$ to both sides of \eqref{eq:bridge} and use $\DynReg_T = \CapReg_T + \sum_t \Dstruct_t$.
\end{proof}

Two points distinguish this from a restatement of optimism. First, the certified quantity is $\CapReg_T$, regret against the best policy the learner could represent, which is what a bounded agent can actually drive down; the global $\DynReg_T$ carries the additive structural term $\sum_t \Dstruct_t$ that \emph{no} $K_L$-bit learner can remove, and we display it rather than hide it inside the comparator. Second, $\rho_t$ is not defined as this bound; it is the intrinsic object of Definition~\ref{def:exreg}, and \eqref{eq:bridge} is a property it acquires \emph{only} under soundness. The decomposition itself abstracts the optimism gap of OFU analyses \citep{jaksch2010ucrl2}; the content is in which examiners are sound and consistent, addressed next.

\section{Worked Case: the Stochastic Bandit}
\label{sec:bandit}

\paragraph{Setting.} $K$ arms, reward $r \in \{0,1\}$ with $\Pr(r{=}1 \mid a) = \mu_a$; one state, $H=1$. If $K_L \ge \log_2 K$ the learner can index every arm, so $\PiK$ is all constant-arm policies and $f^{K_L}_t = \mu^{*} := \max_a \mu_a$ with $\Dstruct_t = 0$; if $K_L < \log_2 K$ the learner cannot even name all arms and $\Dstruct_t > 0$ in general, a first concrete instance of the structural gap. Take the full-capacity case. With $N_a(t)$, $\hat\mu_a(t)$ the pull-count and empirical mean and $c_a(t) := \sqrt{2\log t / N_a(t-1)}$, set $\Vhi_t = \max_a[\hat\mu_a(t-1)+c_a(t)]$, $\Vlow_t = \hat\mu_{a_t}(t-1)-c_{a_t}(t)$, and let $\mathcal{L}$ be UCB1.

\begin{proposition}[Soundness and recovery of UCB1]
\label{prop:bandit}
The bandit examiner is sound with $\delta_t = 0$ at per-round failure probability at most $2t^{-3}$, and $\Ex[\Rex_T] = O(\sqrt{KT\log T})$. Hence by Theorem~\ref{thm:bridge}, $\Ex[\CapReg_T] = O(\sqrt{KT\log T})$, recovering \citet{auer2002ucb}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Conditioned on $N_a(t-1)=n$, arm $a$'s rewards are i.i.d.\ in $[0,1]$ with mean $\mu_a$, so Hoeffding's inequality gives $\Pr(|\hat\mu_a(t-1)-\mu_a| \ge c_a(t) \mid N_a(t-1){=}n) \le 2e^{-2nc_a(t)^2} = 2t^{-4}$. A union bound over the $K \le t$ arms gives, with probability $\ge 1-2t^{-3}$, $\mu_a \le \hat\mu_a(t-1)+c_a(t)$ and $\hat\mu_a(t-1)-c_a(t) \le \mu_a$ for all $a$. The first at the maximizing arm yields $\Vhi_t \ge \mu^{*} = f^{K_L}_t$ (so (S2) with $\delta_t=0$); the second at $a_t$ yields $\Vlow_t \le \mu_{a_t} = f^{\pi_t}_t$ (so (S1)). Since UCB1 pulls the upper-index maximizer, $\Vhi_t = \hat\mu_{a_t}(t-1)+c_{a_t}(t)$, so $\rho_t = 2c_{a_t}(t) = 2\sqrt{2\log t / N_{a_t}(t-1)}$. Grouping by arm, using $\sum_{n=1}^{N}n^{-1/2} \le 2\sqrt{N}$ and Cauchy--Schwarz ($\sum_a \sqrt{N_a(T)} \le \sqrt{KT}$),
\[
\Rex_T \le 2\sqrt{2\log T}\sum_{a}\sum_{n=1}^{N_a(T)} n^{-1/2} \le 4\sqrt{2\log T}\sum_a \sqrt{N_a(T)} \le 4\sqrt{2}\,\sqrt{KT\log T}. \qedhere
\]
\end{proof}

Here $\rho_t$ is exactly twice the UCB confidence width, and the within-capacity regret it certifies coincides with ordinary bandit regret because the learner can represent every arm. The under-capacity case $K_L < \log_2 K$ is where examiner regret and ordinary regret diverge: the agent can certify it is doing as well as its $K_L$ bits allow, even though a structural gap to $\mu^{*}$ remains.

\section{The Limit of Self-Evaluation}
\label{sec:limits}

Soundness is a condition, and in a big world it is not free. The next result shows that bounded capacity forces a dilemma: an honest examiner cannot certify completion.

\begin{theorem}[Contented ignorance]
\label{thm:ignorance}
Fix $\epsilon \in (0,\tfrac12)$ and an integer $N \ge 2$. There is a family of $N$ bandit environments $\{\mu_i\}_{i=1}^{N}$ and a learner $\pi$ such that the history $h_t$ has the same law under every $\mu_i$, and any examiner that is a measurable function of the history and is sound on the whole family with slack $\delta_t$ satisfies, on every $\mu_i$ and for all $t$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ignorance}
\rho_t \;\ge\; \epsilon - \delta_t .
\end{equation}
In particular, on the environment $\mu_1$ in which $\pi$ is optimal (so $\CapReg = \DynReg = 0$), a consistent examiner reports $\rho_t \ge \epsilon - o(1) > 0$ indefinitely; conversely, any examiner that drives $\rho_t \to 0$ violates soundness on some $\mu_i$, $i \ge 2$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Construction.} Take $K \ge N$ arms with $K_L \ge \log_2 K$ (so $\PiK$ is all arms and $f^{K_L} = f^{*}$). In $\mu_1$ every arm has mean $\tfrac12$, so arm $1$ is optimal and $f^{K_L}_{\mu_1} = \tfrac12$. For $i \ge 2$, $\mu_i$ agrees with $\mu_1$ except arm $i$ has mean $\tfrac12 + \epsilon$, so $f^{K_L}_{\mu_i} = \tfrac12 + \epsilon$. Let $\pi$ pull arm $1$ forever; then $f^{\pi}_{\mu_i,t} = \tfrac12$ for all $i$.

\emph{Step 1 (identical histories).} Under $\pi$ only arm $1$ is observed, and its reward is $\mathrm{Bernoulli}(\tfrac12)$ in every $\mu_i$. Hence the law of $h_t$ is identical across all $\mu_i$; realizing them on a common probability space through the shared history, the examiner output $(\Vhi_t,\Vlow_t)$, a fixed measurable function of $h_t$, is the \emph{same random variable} under every $\mu_i$.

\emph{Step 2 (guarantee).} Since $f^{\pi}_{\mu_i,t} = \tfrac12$, (S1) gives $\Vlow_t \le \tfrac12$ on the soundness event.

\emph{Step 3 (aspiration).} Since $\Vhi_t$ does not depend on $i$ while (S2) must hold for each $\mu_i$, on the soundness event $\Vhi_t \ge \max_{i}(f^{K_L}_{\mu_i} - \delta_t) = \tfrac12 + \epsilon - \delta_t$.

\emph{Step 4 (gap).} Combining, $\rho_t = \Vhi_t - \Vlow_t \ge \epsilon - \delta_t$ on every $\mu_i$, which is \eqref{eq:ignorance}. If $\delta_t \to 0$ then $\rho_t \ge \epsilon - o(1)$ on $\mu_1$ where the true regret is $0$; if instead $\rho_t \to 0$ then $\Vhi_t \le \Vlow_t + o(1) \le \tfrac12 + o(1)$, below $f^{K_L}_{\mu_i} - \delta_t = \tfrac12 + \epsilon - o(1)$ for $i \ge 2$, contradicting (S2). \textbf{[proved]}
\end{proof}

The obstruction is informational, not algorithmic: because the learner never pulls the alternative arms, no history-based examiner can distinguish $\mu_1$ from $\mu_i$, so a single sound aspiration must hedge against the best hidden alternative and $\rho_t$ cannot fall below $\epsilon - \delta_t$. Escaping the trap requires the \emph{learner} to explore the alternatives, so histories differ across environments, and the \emph{examiner} to have enough capacity to represent them. This is where the allocation $K = K_L + K_E$ binds, and it is the precise sense in which, in a big world, self-evaluation is limited by exploration and capacity rather than by cleverness.

\paragraph{The capacity-allocation question.}
Theorems~\ref{thm:bridge} and \ref{thm:ignorance} bracket the problem: soundness makes $\rho_t$ a certificate, and bounded capacity can make soundness-with-small-$\rho_t$ unattainable. Between them sits a budgeting problem. With total $K = K_L + K_E$, bits spent on the learner widen $\PiK$ (shrinking $\Dstruct$) but leave fewer for an examiner that can explore-and-represent alternatives; bits spent on the examiner tighten certification but cap what the learner can express. We conjecture the optimal split $K_E^{*}$ depends on environment complexity and is generally interior, and we regard its characterization as the central problem this framework opens. It is distinctively continual: a one-time examiner suffices for a fixed task, but under never-ending non-stationarity the examiner must keep paying for itself.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\textbf{Foundations of CRL and bounded rationality.} \citet{abel2023definition} define CRL as never-ending learning; \citet{abel2024dogmas,abel2025rethinking,abel2020bounded} develop its foundations and the bounded-rationality view of RL agents. Bounded optimality \citep{russell1995bounded}, computational rationality \citep{lewis2014computational}, and resource-rational analysis \citep{lieder2020resource} establish the principle we adopt: a bounded agent should be measured against the best use of its resources, which we instantiate as the capacity-constrained comparator of Definition~\ref{def:capcomp}. \textbf{Big-world hypothesis.} \citet{javed2025bigworld,lewandowski2025bigger} argue the agent is far smaller than its world; our split formalizes one consequence for evaluation. \textbf{Learning progress and intrinsic motivation.} Compression- and learning-progress signals \citep{schmidhuber2010formal,oudeyer2007intrinsic} are self-assessed drives for exploration; examiner regret is a value-unit, capacity-indexed relative of these, but tied to a soundness condition that makes it a certificate rather than only a drive. \textbf{Capacity-bounded RL.} \citet{lu2021bitbybit} cast capacity in bits; \citet{arumugam2024capacity} build rate-distortion-projected posteriors, our canonical $K_E$-bounded examiner, and define within-capacity satisficing targets analogous to $f^{K_L}$. \textbf{Bayes-adaptive and universal RL.} BAMDP \citep{guez2012bamcp,lidayan2025bampf} supplies the canonical examiner; AIXI \citep{hutter2005aixi} is its uncomputable limit. \textbf{Regret and certification.} OFU analyses \citep{jaksch2010ucrl2,auer2002ucb} use the optimism gap our bridge abstracts; PAC-MDP \citep{strehl2009pacmdp} supplies value bounds meeting (S1)--(S2); calibration \citep{foster1998calibration} and KWIK \citep{li2011kwik} formalize honest self-assessment; non-stationary regret \citep{wei2021master} controls $\delta_t$ under drift; hindsight rationality \citep{morrill2021hindsight} varies the comparator class as we vary $\PiK$. Across these the evaluator is implicit or fused with the policy; we make the examiner a first-class, capacity-bounded module and study the measure it computes.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

We have proposed examiner regret as an intrinsic, capacity-constrained measure of learning, defined from the agent's internal state and studied first as an object in its own right (Proposition~\ref{prop:intrinsic}), then certified against the best policy the agent could represent under a soundness condition (Theorem~\ref{thm:bridge}), with the structural gap to global optimality displayed, and finally bounded by an impossibility that exposes the limit of self-evaluation in a big world (Theorem~\ref{thm:ignorance}). The framework reframes evaluation in continual RL as the allocation of a fixed bit budget between acting and judging. It opens a concrete program: characterize the optimal split $K_E^{*}$ for structured big worlds; design examiners that remain sound under drift; and test empirically whether separating actor and examiner capacity, rather than sharing it, improves continual-RL benchmarks at fixed total parameter count. We claim no new algorithm and no tight bound beyond prior work; the value is in starting from what the agent can compute about itself, and in the question that starting point makes precise.
