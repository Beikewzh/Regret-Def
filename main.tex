\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Continual reinforcement learning (CRL) studies agents that never stop learning \citep{abel2023definition}, a view forced by the \emph{big-world hypothesis}: the agent is far smaller than its world and cannot represent, learn, or retain a fixed optimal solution \citep{javed2025bigworld,lewandowski2025bigger}. We ask a question this picture makes unavoidable but that standard theory does not face: \emph{who evaluates such an agent?} Regret, our usual measure of learning, compares the agent to an optimum $V^{*}$ obtained from a model, prior, or comparator class supplied from outside \citep{lai1985asymptotically,jaksch2010ucrl2}. A continual agent in a big world has no such outside source, and would not be well served by one even if it did: bounded, it cannot represent the optimal policy, so the right benchmark is the best use of its own resources, not an external ideal \citep{russell1995bounded,lewis2014computational,lieder2020resource}.

\paragraph{Position.} We take the position that, in CRL, \emph{there is no external examiner}. Whatever certifies the agent's progress must be computed by a bounded module inside the agent, drawing on the same finite capacity as acting. The meaningful notion of regret is therefore not what an idealized outside observer could measure, but what such an internal, capacity-bounded examiner can certify. Our central claim is that this self-evaluation is governed by \emph{coverage}: an agent can honestly certify its own progress to the extent that its experience can cover the part of the world that matters, and in a big world, where the world outruns experience, honest self-evaluation is \emph{necessarily} costly, for every agent and not merely for a careless one.

\paragraph{What we do.} We formalize the internal evaluator as an \emph{examiner} that, alongside the acting \emph{learner}, forecasts an \emph{aspiration} $\Vhi$ and a \emph{guarantee} $\Vlow$ on the agent's own value; the gap $\rho_t := \Vhi - \Vlow$ is the \emph{examiner regret}. The learner and examiner hold separate budgets $K_L$ and $K_E$, with $K = K_L + K_E$. We then argue the position in three results. A self-report is free and so guarantees nothing on its own (Proposition~\ref{prop:nothing}); a \emph{soundness} condition turns it into a certificate of the regret a bounded agent can actually reduce (Lemma~\ref{lem:bridge}), and when experience covers the world this certificate is tight, recovering the classical bandit rate (Proposition~\ref{prop:bandit}); but when the world outruns experience, no agent can keep its honest self-report small (Theorem~\ref{thm:price}). We close with how $\rho_t$ can be estimated and used, and with the limitations a reviewer should weigh. We claim no new algorithm or rate; the contribution is the position, the object that makes it precise, and a lower bound that shows the position has teeth.

\section{A Learner That Acts and an Examiner That Judges}
\label{sec:defs}

\paragraph{Setting.} An agent interacts with an unknown environment over a single, never-resetting stream; we assume no MDP, POMDP, or stationarity structure. Rewards are bounded, $r_t \in [0,\Rmax]$, value is measured over an $H$-step window, and the agent is capacity-bounded. We seek a definition of a learning measure computable from the agent's internal state, the conditions under which it certifies learning, and the limit beyond which it cannot.

\begin{definition}[Stream and history]
\label{def:stream}
A \emph{stream} is a sequence $\{(o_t, r_t, a_t)\}_{t \ge 0}$ with $o_t \in \mathcal{O}$, $r_t \in [0,\Rmax]$, $a_t \in \Acts$. The \emph{history} is $h_t := (o_0, r_0, a_1, \dots, a_t, o_t, r_t) \in \Hist_t$. By Ionescu--Tulcea the kernels extend to a measure $\mu$ on $(\mathcal{O}\times\mathbb{R}\times\Acts)^{\infty}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Policy and value]
\label{def:value}
A \emph{policy} is $\pi : \Hist \to \Delta(\Acts)$, with value $f^{\pi}_t := \Ex_{\mu, \pi}\big[\sum_{s=t+1}^{t+H} r_s \mid h_t\big]$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Capacity-constrained comparator]
\label{def:capcomp}
Let $\Theta_L$ be the learner's parameter set, $|\Theta_L| \le 2^{K_L}$, and $\pi_\theta$ the policy encoded by $\theta$. Write $\PiK := \{\pi_\theta : \theta \in \Theta_L\}$. The \emph{capacity-constrained optimum} is $f^{K_L}_t := \sup_{\pi \in \PiK} f^{\pi}_t$, and the \emph{structural gap} is $\Dstruct_t := f^{*}_t - f^{K_L}_t \ge 0$ with $f^{*}_t := \sup_{\pi} f^{\pi}_t$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Learner--examiner agent]
\label{def:agent}
An \emph{agent} is a pair $\mathcal{A} = (\mathcal{L}, \mathcal{E})$. The \emph{learner} $\mathcal{L} : \Hist_t \to \Delta(\Acts)$ emits $\pi_t \in \PiK$. The \emph{examiner} $\mathcal{E} = (U, L)$, with $U, L : \Hist_t \to \mathbb{R}$ and $U \ge L$ pointwise, emits aspiration $\Vhi := U(h_t)$ and guarantee $\Vlow := L(h_t)$, $|\Theta_E| \le 2^{K_E}$. Total capacity $K := K_L + K_E$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Examiner regret]
\label{def:exreg}
$\rho_t := \Vhi - \Vlow \ge 0$, with $\Rex_T := \sum_{t=1}^T \rho_t$; a function of $h_t$ and $\theta^E_t$ alone, with no reference to $\mu$, $f^{\pi}$, or any optimum.
\end{definition}

\section{A Self-Report Carries No Guarantee}
\label{sec:nothing}

The definition is deliberately cheap, and that is the first thing to confront.

\begin{proposition}[Examiner regret is uninformative on its own]
\label{prop:nothing}
There is an examiner $\mathcal{E}_0$ with $\rho_t \equiv 0$ for every learner and stream. Hence two agents on the same stream can share the trajectory $\rho_t \equiv 0$ while one has $\CapReg_T := \sum_t (f^{K_L}_t - f^{\pi_t}_t) = 0$ and the other has $\CapReg_T = \Omega(T)$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Let $\mathcal{E}_0$ output $U \equiv L \equiv 0$, so $\rho_t \equiv 0$ regardless of learner or environment. Pair it with a learner playing an optimum of $\PiK$ ($\CapReg_T = 0$), and separately with one playing value $f^{K_L}_t - c$ each step ($\CapReg_T = cT$). Both yield $\rho_t \equiv 0$.
\end{proof}

Examiner regret therefore carries no guarantee by itself; any informativeness must come from a condition tying the examiner's outputs to value. That condition is extrinsic and costs capacity, and the rest of the paper is about it and its price.

\section{Soundness Turns the Self-Report Into a Certificate}
\label{sec:bridge}

\begin{definition}[Soundness and consistency]
\label{def:sound}
$\mathcal{E}$ is \emph{sound} on $[1,T]$ with slack $(\delta_t)$, $\delta_t \ge 0$, if with probability at least $1-\eta$, for all $t \le T$, (S1) $\Vlow \le f^{\pi_t}_t$ and (S2) $\Vhi \ge f^{K_L}_t - \delta_t$; it is \emph{consistent} if also (S3) $\Gamma_T := \sum_t \delta_t = o(T)$.
\end{definition}

(S1) makes the guarantee a true lower bound on the current policy's value; (S2) makes the aspiration optimistic for the best policy the learner could represent, the resource-rational comparator \citep{russell1995bounded,lieder2020resource}. The empty examiner $\mathcal{E}_0$ of Proposition~\ref{prop:nothing} fails (S2) whenever $f^{K_L}_t > \delta_t$, which is how soundness excludes the vacuous self-report.

\begin{lemma}[Soundness bridge]
\label{lem:bridge}
If $\mathcal{E}$ is sound with slack $(\delta_t)$ on $[1,T]$, then with probability $\ge 1-2\eta$,
\[
\CapReg_T \le \Rex_T + \Gamma_T,
\qquad
\DynReg_T := \sum_t (f^{*}_t - f^{\pi_t}_t) \le \Rex_T + \Gamma_T + \sum_t \Dstruct_t .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For each $t$, $f^{K_L}_t - f^{\pi_t}_t = (f^{K_L}_t - \Vhi) + (\Vhi - \Vlow) + (\Vlow - f^{\pi_t}_t)$; on the joint event of (S1),(S2) (probability $\ge 1-2\eta$ by the union bound) the terms are $\le \delta_t$, $=\rho_t$, $\le 0$. Sum over $t$; add $\sum_t \Dstruct_t$ for the second inequality.
\end{proof}

We are explicit that this is a \emph{decomposition}: the identity is the optimism-gap step of OFU analyses \citep{jaksch2010ucrl2}, not a new rate. Its role is to say what a \emph{sound} examiner regret means, namely an upper bound on the regret a bounded agent can actually reduce, $\CapReg_T$, with the unremovable structural cost $\sum_t \Dstruct_t$ displayed rather than hidden. The substance is whether soundness holds cheaply, which we now show turns on coverage.

\paragraph{When experience covers the world, the certificate is tight.}
Consider $K$ Bernoulli arms with $K_L \ge \log_2 K$, so $\PiK$ is all arms and $\Dstruct_t = 0$. Let $c_a(t) = \sqrt{2\log t / N_a(t-1)}$, $\Vhi = \max_a[\hat\mu_a + c_a(t)]$, $\Vlow = \hat\mu_{a_t} - c_{a_t}(t)$, with $\mathcal{L} = $ UCB1.

\begin{proposition}[Coverable case: recovery of UCB1]
\label{prop:bandit}
The bandit examiner is sound with $\delta_t = 0$ at per-round failure probability $\le 2t^{-3}$, and $\Ex[\Rex_T] = O(\sqrt{KT\log T})$; hence by Lemma~\ref{lem:bridge}, $\Ex[\CapReg_T] = O(\sqrt{KT\log T})$ \citep{auer2002ucb}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Conditioned on $N_a(t-1)=n$, arm $a$'s rewards are i.i.d.\ in $[0,1]$ with mean $\mu_a$, so Hoeffding gives $\Pr(|\hat\mu_a - \mu_a| \ge c_a(t)) \le 2t^{-4}$; a union bound over $K \le t$ arms gives, w.p.\ $\ge 1-2t^{-3}$, $\mu_a \le \hat\mu_a + c_a(t)$ and $\hat\mu_a - c_a(t) \le \mu_a$ for all $a$, i.e.\ (S2) at the best arm and (S1) at $a_t$, with $\delta_t = 0$. As UCB1 pulls the upper-index maximizer, $\rho_t = 2c_{a_t}(t)$; grouping by arm with $\sum_{n\le N} n^{-1/2} \le 2\sqrt N$ and Cauchy--Schwarz ($\sum_a \sqrt{N_a(T)} \le \sqrt{KT}$) gives $\Rex_T \le 4\sqrt{2}\sqrt{KT\log T}$.
\end{proof}

Here the learner pulls every arm, so experience eventually covers the world, the slack vanishes, and the honest self-report converges to the truth. This is the favourable regime; the big-world regime is the opposite one.

\section{In a Big World, Honest Self-Evaluation Is Unavoidably Costly}
\label{sec:price}

When the world offers more to discover than the agent has time to probe, coverage fails and the certificate cannot be cheap, for any agent.

\begin{theorem}[Price of self-evaluation under incomplete coverage]
\label{thm:price}
Fix $\epsilon \in (0,\tfrac12)$, $N \ge 2$, horizon $T < N$, and $K_L \ge \log_2 N$. Consider the family of $N$-armed Bernoulli bandits $\{\mu_i\}_{i=1}^N$ in which, under $\mu_i$, arm $i$ has mean $\tfrac12 + \epsilon$ and all other arms have mean $\tfrac12$ (so $f^{K_L}_{\mu_i} = f^{*}_{\mu_i} = \tfrac12 + \epsilon$ and $\Dstruct \equiv 0$). For \emph{any} agent $\mathcal{A} = (\mathcal{L},\mathcal{E})$ whose examiner is sound on the family with slack $(\delta_t)$ and failure probability $\eta$, there is an environment $\mu_{i^{*}}$ in the family on which, with probability at least $1-2\eta$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:price}
\Rex_T \;=\; \sum_{t=1}^T \rho_t \;\ge\; \epsilon\,T - \Gamma_T,
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
\DynReg_T = \epsilon\,T .
\end{equation}
Consequently, on the worst-case environment, the examiner is either inconsistent ($\Gamma_T = \Omega(T)$) or carries linear cumulative examiner regret ($\Rex_T = \Omega(T)$): honest self-evaluation cannot be both consistent and small.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Null run and choice of $i^{*}$.} Let $\mu_0$ be the all-$\tfrac12$ bandit. Run $\mathcal{A}$ in $\mu_0$ and let $A_T$ be the (random) set of distinct arms pulled in $[1,T]$. Since one arm is pulled per step, $|A_T| \le T < N$, so there exists $i^{*} \in [N]\setminus A_T$.

\emph{Coupling.} Couple the runs in $\mu_0$ and $\mu_{i^{*}}$ on the same internal randomness. The two environments differ only at arm $i^{*}$, which is not pulled in the $\mu_0$ run; all pulled arms have identical reward law ($\mathrm{Bernoulli}(\tfrac12)$) in both. By induction on $t$, the action and observation sequences coincide, so arm $i^{*}$ is never pulled in $\mu_{i^{*}}$ either, and the history, hence the examiner output $(\Vhi,\Vlow)$, is identical to the $\mu_0$ run.

\emph{Bounds under $\mu_{i^{*}}$.} The learner never pulls the optimal arm $i^{*}$, so $f^{\pi_t}_{\mu_{i^{*}},t} = \tfrac12$ and $\DynReg_T = \sum_t (\tfrac12+\epsilon - \tfrac12) = \epsilon T$. On the soundness event, (S1) gives $\Vlow \le \tfrac12$ and (S2) gives $\Vhi \ge f^{K_L}_{\mu_{i^{*}}} - \delta_t = \tfrac12 + \epsilon - \delta_t$, so $\rho_t \ge \epsilon - \delta_t$ for all $t$. Summing gives $\Rex_T \ge \epsilon T - \Gamma_T$, which is \eqref{eq:price}. The dichotomy follows: if $\Gamma_T = o(T)$ then $\Rex_T \ge \epsilon T - o(T) = \Omega(T)$, otherwise $\Gamma_T = \Omega(T)$. \textbf{[proved]}
\end{proof}

The statement is worst-case over the family, in the manner of Le~Cam two-point lower bounds \citep{lai1985asymptotically}: there is an environment, not every environment, on which the agent both performs poorly and cannot honestly say so. Two features matter for the position. First, it holds for \emph{any} learner: exploration helps only up to the $T$ arms it can reach, and when $N > T$ some niche is always missed, so the obstruction is coverage, not carelessness. Second, it makes consistency's role explicit (a common worry about assumptions like (S3)): in a big world the agent cannot have both a consistent examiner and a vanishing self-report, so a small $\rho_t$ in such a setting should be read as overconfidence, not success.

\paragraph{The capacity-allocation question.}
Proposition~\ref{prop:bandit} and Theorem~\ref{thm:price} are the two sides of coverage, and between them sits a budgeting problem. Bits spent on the learner ($K_L$) widen $\PiK$ and let it represent and reach more of the world, shrinking the missed-coverage set; bits spent on the examiner ($K_E$) let it represent what coverage reveals and certify tighter. With $T < N$ no allocation removes the $\Omega(T)$ price, but the allocation governs how quickly coverage grows and how tight the certificate is once it does. Characterizing the optimal split $K_E^{*}$ as a function of how fast the world is discovered is, in our view, the central open problem the position raises.

\section{Estimating and Using Examiner Regret}
\label{sec:practical}

The construction is directly estimable, which is what makes the position more than descriptive. \textbf{[heuristic]} A practical examiner estimates the aspiration $\Vhi$ as an optimistic value, the upper quantile or maximum head of a value-function ensemble or a UCB-style $Q$-estimate, and the guarantee $\Vlow$ as a pessimistic value, the lower quantile or a conservative critic \citep{lu2021bitbybit,arumugam2024capacity}; $\rho_t$ is then the ensemble's value-uncertainty gap, computed online with no access to $V^{*}$. This gives three uses. As an \emph{exploration and learning-progress signal}, acting to reduce $\rho_t$ drives the agent toward states where aspiration and guarantee disagree, the lineage of compression- and learning-progress drives \citep{schmidhuber2010formal,oudeyer2007intrinsic} but in value units with a soundness target. As a \emph{model-agnostic comparison metric}, running different algorithms on one continual stream and comparing $\rho_t$ trajectories estimates which is nearer its own capacity-constrained optimum, without $V^{*}$. And because soundness reduces to the calibration of the ensemble, a practitioner can \emph{audit} whether a low $\rho_t$ is trustworthy or the empty report of Proposition~\ref{prop:nothing}. We do not run these experiments; we note the path because it is what would turn the position into a tool.

\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limits-disc}

\textbf{(L1) The content is in soundness, not in $\rho_t$} (Proposition~\ref{prop:nothing}); the intrinsic object certifies nothing alone, and soundness is extrinsic, requiring the examiner to relate outputs to value via, e.g., a posterior over a model class. The model-free part is only the self-report. \textbf{(L2) The bridge is a decomposition} (Lemma~\ref{lem:bridge} is the OFU optimism gap), not a new rate; its role is interpretive. \textbf{(L3) The positive, tight regime is shown only for the full-capacity stationary bandit} (Proposition~\ref{prop:bandit}); soundness of a capacity-bounded examiner under genuine non-stationarity, and the under-capacity case $\Dstruct_t > 0$, are not established. \textbf{[GAP]} \textbf{(L4) The lower bound is worst-case and coverage-driven}: Theorem~\ref{thm:price} exhibits a hard environment under $T < N$; it does not bound a fixed environment, and the favourable $T \ge N$ regime escapes it. \textbf{(L5) No formal learner--examiner coupling}: $\mathcal{L}$ and $\mathcal{E}$ are separate maps with no feedback channel, so the allocation $K_E^{*}$ is conceptual, with no mechanism in the present formalism. \textbf{(L6) The capacity model is coarse}, counting representable policies ($|\Theta_L| \le 2^{K_L}$) and ignoring computation. \textbf{(L7) Novelty over rate-distortion satisficing} \citep{arumugam2024capacity} is the explicit examiner module, the self-report-certifies-nothing thesis (Proposition~\ref{prop:nothing}), and the coverage lower bound (Theorem~\ref{thm:price}), rather than a new upper bound. \textbf{(L8) No experiments}; the practical recipe of Section~\ref{sec:practical} is untested.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\textbf{Foundations and bounded rationality.} \citet{abel2023definition,abel2024dogmas,abel2020bounded} define and develop CRL and the bounded-rationality view; bounded optimality \citep{russell1995bounded}, computational rationality \citep{lewis2014computational}, and resource-rational analysis \citep{lieder2020resource} motivate measuring a bounded agent against its own resources, our comparator $f^{K_L}$. \textbf{Big-world hypothesis.} \citet{javed2025bigworld,lewandowski2025bigger}. \textbf{Learning progress.} \citet{schmidhuber2010formal,oudeyer2007intrinsic} give self-assessed drives; $\rho_t$ is a value-unit relative with a soundness target. \textbf{Capacity-bounded RL.} \citet{lu2021bitbybit} cast capacity in bits; \citet{arumugam2024capacity} build $K_E$-bounded rate-distortion posteriors with within-capacity targets analogous to $f^{K_L}$, the closest prior work. \textbf{Bayes-adaptive / universal RL.} \citet{guez2012bamcp,lidayan2025bampf,hutter2005aixi}. \textbf{Regret, calibration, certification.} OFU \citep{jaksch2010ucrl2,auer2002ucb}, PAC-MDP \citep{strehl2009pacmdp}, calibration \citep{foster1998calibration}, KWIK \citep{li2011kwik}, non-stationary regret \citep{wei2021master}, hindsight rationality \citep{morrill2021hindsight}. Across these the evaluator is external or fused with the policy; we make it an explicit, capacity-bounded module and ask what its self-report can be worth.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

We have argued that continual reinforcement learning has no external examiner, so the regret that matters is what a bounded internal examiner can certify, and that this self-evaluation is governed by coverage: tight when experience covers the world (Proposition~\ref{prop:bandit}), and unavoidably costly when the world outruns experience (Theorem~\ref{thm:price}), with the empty self-report ruled out by soundness (Proposition~\ref{prop:nothing}). The picture turns evaluation in CRL into a budgeting problem between acting and judging, and the object is directly estimable as a learning-progress and comparison signal. We claim no new algorithm or rate; the contribution is the position and a lower bound that gives it teeth, and the questions it sharpens, foremost how a well-designed joint agent should split its bits between acting and judging when neither can be made complete.
