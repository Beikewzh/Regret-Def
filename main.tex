\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Continual reinforcement learning (CRL) studies agents that never stop learning \citep{abel2023definition}, a stance motivated by the \emph{big-world hypothesis}: the agent is far smaller than its world, so it cannot represent, learn, or retain a fixed optimal solution \citep{javed2025bigworld,lewandowski2025bigger}. Such an agent is permanently and irreducibly bounded. Our standard instrument for certifying that it is learning, regret, sits awkwardly with this picture. Regret is the cumulative cost of not knowing the environment, measured against an optimum $V^{*}$ \citep{lai1985asymptotically,jaksch2010ucrl2}, and obtaining $V^{*}$ requires a model, a prior, or a comparator class supplied from outside. In a big world none is available; and even if $V^{*}$ were known, it would be the wrong target, since a bounded agent cannot represent the optimal policy and so should be judged against the best use of its own resources \citep{russell1995bounded,lewis2014computational,lieder2020resource}.

A tempting fix is to let the agent score itself: have it report a self-assessed value gap and call that its regret. We make this idea precise, but we begin from its central difficulty rather than hide it. \emph{A self-report is free, and therefore certifies nothing on its own.} An agent can declare its gap to be zero and stop; nothing in the report forbids it. The real question is not whether an agent can name an internal regret, but \emph{what must be added to that report, and at what cost, to make it trustworthy.} This paper studies that cost.

We split the agent into a \emph{learner}, which acts, and an \emph{examiner}, which forecasts an \emph{aspiration} $\Vhi$ for the best value it believes attainable and a \emph{guarantee} $\Vlow$ for the value it is confident it achieves. We call $\rho_t := \Vhi - \Vlow$ the \emph{examiner regret}. Each module holds a separate budget in bits, $K_L$ and $K_E$, with $K = K_L + K_E$. We then proceed in four honest steps. (1) We show $\rho_t$ alone is uninformative: two agents with identical $\rho_t \equiv 0$ can be optimal or arbitrarily suboptimal (Proposition~\ref{prop:nothing}). (2) We isolate the missing ingredient as a \emph{soundness} condition relating $\Vhi, \Vlow$ to value, and show that under it $\rho_t$ upper-bounds the agent's regret against the best policy it could itself represent (Theorem~\ref{thm:bridge}); the stochastic bandit recovers the classical rate. (3) We show soundness is not free in a big world: for an agent that does not explore, no examiner of any capacity can drive $\rho_t$ to zero while staying sound (Theorem~\ref{thm:cost}). (4) We sketch how $\rho_t$ could be estimated and used in practice. We claim no new algorithm or rate; the contribution is a capacity-aware notion of regret and a clear account of the price of trusting it.

\section{Setting and Definitions}
\label{sec:defs}

\paragraph{Setting.} An agent interacts with an unknown environment over a single, never-resetting stream; we assume no MDP, POMDP, or stationarity structure. Rewards are bounded, $r_t \in [0,\Rmax]$, and value is measured over a look-ahead window of $H$ steps. The agent is capacity-bounded. We seek a \emph{definition} of a learning measure computable from the agent's internal state, the conditions under which it certifies learning, and the limits beyond which it cannot.

\begin{definition}[Stream and history]
\label{def:stream}
A \emph{stream} is a sequence $\{(o_t, r_t, a_t)\}_{t \ge 0}$ with $o_t \in \mathcal{O}$, $r_t \in [0,\Rmax]$, $a_t \in \Acts$. The \emph{history} is $h_t := (o_0, r_0, a_1, \dots, a_t, o_t, r_t) \in \Hist_t$. By Ionescu--Tulcea the kernels extend to a measure $\mu$ on $(\mathcal{O}\times\mathbb{R}\times\Acts)^{\infty}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Policy and value]
\label{def:value}
A \emph{policy} is $\pi : \Hist \to \Delta(\Acts)$, with value $f^{\pi}_t := \Ex_{\mu, \pi}\big[\sum_{s=t+1}^{t+H} r_s \mid h_t\big]$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Capacity-constrained comparator]
\label{def:capcomp}
Let $\Theta_L$ be the learner's parameter set, $|\Theta_L| \le 2^{K_L}$, and $\pi_\theta$ the policy encoded by $\theta \in \Theta_L$. Write $\PiK := \{\pi_\theta : \theta \in \Theta_L\}$ for the policies the learner can represent. The \emph{capacity-constrained optimum} is $f^{K_L}_t := \sup_{\pi \in \PiK} f^{\pi}_t$, and the \emph{structural gap} is $\Dstruct_t := f^{*}_t - f^{K_L}_t \ge 0$, where $f^{*}_t := \sup_{\pi} f^{\pi}_t$.
\end{definition}

The structural gap is the part of optimality a $K_L$-bit learner can never reach. We keep it visible rather than absorbing it into the comparator.

\begin{definition}[Learner--examiner agent]
\label{def:agent}
An \emph{agent} is a pair $\mathcal{A} = (\mathcal{L}, \mathcal{E})$. The \emph{learner} $\mathcal{L} : \Hist_t \to \Delta(\Acts)$ emits $\pi_t \in \PiK$ ($|\Theta_L| \le 2^{K_L}$). The \emph{examiner} $\mathcal{E} = (U, L)$, with $U, L : \Hist_t \to \mathbb{R}$ and $U \ge L$ pointwise, emits the aspiration $\Vhi := U(h_t)$ and guarantee $\Vlow := L(h_t)$ ($|\Theta_E| \le 2^{K_E}$). Total capacity $K := K_L + K_E$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Examiner regret]
\label{def:exreg}
$\rho_t := \Vhi - \Vlow \ge 0$, with $\Rex_T := \sum_{t=1}^T \rho_t$. It is a function of $h_t$ and $\theta^E_t$ alone, with no reference to $\mu$, $f^{\pi}$, or any optimum.
\end{definition}

\section{Step 1: The Self-Report Alone Certifies Nothing}
\label{sec:nothing}

We first state plainly the difficulty that shapes the rest of the paper.

\begin{proposition}[$\rho_t$ is uninformative on its own]
\label{prop:nothing}
There is an examiner $\mathcal{E}_0$ such that for \emph{every} learner and every stream, $\rho_t \equiv 0$ for all $t$. Consequently there exist two agents on the same stream with identical examiner-regret trajectories ($\rho_t \equiv 0$), one with $\CapReg_T = 0$ and one with $\CapReg_T = \Omega(T)$, where $\CapReg_T := \sum_t (f^{K_L}_t - f^{\pi_t}_t)$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Let $\mathcal{E}_0$ output $U(h_t) = L(h_t) = 0$ for all $h_t$; then $\rho_t = 0$ identically, irrespective of the learner or environment. Pair $\mathcal{E}_0$ with a learner that plays an optimal policy in $\PiK$ (giving $\CapReg_T = 0$) and, separately, with a learner that plays a policy of value $f^{K_L}_t - c$ for a constant $c > 0$ at each step (giving $\CapReg_T = cT = \Omega(T)$). Both produce $\rho_t \equiv 0$.
\end{proof}

The reading is blunt and is the thesis of the paper: \emph{examiner regret carries no guarantee by itself.} Any informativeness must come from a separate condition that ties the examiner's outputs to actual value. That condition is extrinsic, it costs capacity to maintain, and the remainder of the paper studies it and its price.

\section{Step 2: Soundness, and What It Buys}
\label{sec:bridge}

\begin{definition}[Soundness and consistency]
\label{def:sound}
$\mathcal{E}$ is \emph{sound} on $[1,T]$ with slack $(\delta_t)$, $\delta_t \ge 0$, if with probability at least $1-\eta$, for all $t \le T$,
\[
\text{(S1)}\quad \Vlow \le f^{\pi_t}_t, \qquad\qquad \text{(S2)}\quad \Vhi \ge f^{K_L}_t - \delta_t,
\]
and \emph{consistent} if in addition (S3) $\Gamma_T := \sum_{t=1}^T \delta_t = o(T)$.
\end{definition}

(S1) asks the guarantee to be a true lower bound on the current policy's value; (S2) asks the aspiration to be optimistic for the best policy the learner could represent, the resource-rational comparator \citep{russell1995bounded,lieder2020resource}. The trivial examiner $\mathcal{E}_0$ of Proposition~\ref{prop:nothing} fails (S2) whenever $f^{K_L}_t > \delta_t$, which is exactly how soundness rules out the empty self-report.

\begin{theorem}[Soundness bridge]
\label{thm:bridge}
If $\mathcal{E}$ is sound with slack $(\delta_t)$ on $[1,T]$, then with probability at least $1-2\eta$,
\[
\CapReg_T = \sum_{t=1}^T (f^{K_L}_t - f^{\pi_t}_t) \;\le\; \Rex_T + \Gamma_T,
\qquad
\DynReg_T \;\le\; \Rex_T + \Gamma_T + \sum_{t=1}^T \Dstruct_t .
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For each $t$, $f^{K_L}_t - f^{\pi_t}_t = (f^{K_L}_t - \Vhi) + (\Vhi - \Vlow) + (\Vlow - f^{\pi_t}_t)$. On the joint event of (S1) and (S2) (probability $\ge 1-2\eta$ by the union bound) the first term is $\le \delta_t$, the second is $\rho_t$, the third is $\le 0$; sum over $t$ for the first inequality. Adding $\sum_t \Dstruct_t$ and using $\DynReg_T = \CapReg_T + \sum_t \Dstruct_t$ gives the second.
\end{proof}

We state precisely what this is and is not. It is a \emph{decomposition}: the identity is the optimism-gap step of OFU analyses \citep{jaksch2010ucrl2}, and we claim no new rate. Its content is interpretive: under soundness, the intrinsic $\rho_t$ becomes an upper bound on the regret a bounded agent can actually drive down ($\CapReg_T$), with the unremovable structural cost $\sum_t \Dstruct_t$ shown explicitly. Whether soundness holds, and with what slack, is the substantive question, and we turn to it next; it is also where this framework's limits live (Section~\ref{sec:limits}).

\paragraph{Bandit instance.} For $K$ Bernoulli arms with $K_L \ge \log_2 K$ (so $\PiK$ is all arms and $\Dstruct_t = 0$), let $c_a(t) = \sqrt{2\log t / N_a(t-1)}$, $\Vhi = \max_a[\hat\mu_a + c_a(t)]$, $\Vlow = \hat\mu_{a_t} - c_{a_t}(t)$, with $\mathcal{L} = $ UCB1. Hoeffding's inequality gives soundness with $\delta_t = 0$ at failure probability $\le 2t^{-3}$ per round \citep{auer2002ucb}, and since UCB1 pulls the upper-index maximizer, $\rho_t = 2c_{a_t}(t)$. Grouping by arm with $\sum_{n\le N} n^{-1/2} \le 2\sqrt N$ and Cauchy--Schwarz ($\sum_a \sqrt{N_a(T)} \le \sqrt{KT}$) gives $\Rex_T \le 4\sqrt{2}\sqrt{KT\log T}$, so Theorem~\ref{thm:bridge} recovers the classical $O(\sqrt{KT\log T})$ bound. \textbf{[proved]} This is the full-capacity, stationary case; it is a sanity check that the definition is non-vacuous, not evidence about the big-world regime.

\section{Step 3: Soundness Is Not Free in a Big World}
\label{sec:limits}

The bandit case had $\delta_t = 0$ because the learner explored every arm. We now show that without exploration, soundness forces $\rho_t$ to stay large, so the agent cannot certify that it has finished learning.

\begin{theorem}[The price of trustworthy self-evaluation]
\label{thm:cost}
Fix $\epsilon \in (0,\tfrac12)$ and $N \ge 2$. There is a family of $N$ bandit environments $\{\mu_i\}$ and a learner $\pi$ such that the history $h_t$ has the same law under every $\mu_i$, and any examiner that is a measurable function of the history and is sound on the whole family with slack $\delta_t$ satisfies $\rho_t \ge \epsilon - \delta_t$ for all $t$, on every $\mu_i$. In particular, on the environment $\mu_1$ in which $\pi$ is optimal (regret $0$), a consistent examiner reports $\rho_t \ge \epsilon - o(1) > 0$ indefinitely; and any examiner that drives $\rho_t \to 0$ violates soundness on some $\mu_i$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Take $K \ge N$ arms. In $\mu_1$ all arms have mean $\tfrac12$ (arm $1$ optimal, $f^{K_L}_{\mu_1} = \tfrac12$); for $i \ge 2$, $\mu_i$ differs only in arm $i$ having mean $\tfrac12 + \epsilon$ ($f^{K_L}_{\mu_i} = \tfrac12 + \epsilon$). Let $\pi$ pull arm $1$ forever, so $f^{\pi}_{\mu_i,t} = \tfrac12$. Under $\pi$ only arm $1$ is observed, with reward $\mathrm{Bernoulli}(\tfrac12)$ in every $\mu_i$; hence $h_t$ has the same law across all $\mu_i$, and on a common probability space the output $(\Vhi,\Vlow)$ is the same random variable under every $\mu_i$. By (S1), $\Vlow \le \tfrac12$. Since $\Vhi$ does not depend on $i$ but (S2) holds for each, $\Vhi \ge \max_i (f^{K_L}_{\mu_i} - \delta_t) = \tfrac12 + \epsilon - \delta_t$. Thus $\rho_t \ge \epsilon - \delta_t$ on every $\mu_i$. If $\delta_t \to 0$ then $\rho_t \ge \epsilon - o(1)$ on $\mu_1$ (true regret $0$); if $\rho_t \to 0$ then $\Vhi \le \tfrac12 + o(1) < \tfrac12 + \epsilon - \delta_t = f^{K_L}_{\mu_i} - \delta_t$ for $i \ge 2$ at large $t$, contradicting (S2). \textbf{[proved]}
\end{proof}

The obstruction is informational: because $\pi$ never pulls the other arms, no history-based examiner can tell $\mu_1$ from $\mu_i$, so a sound aspiration must hedge against the best hidden alternative. We are explicit about scope. The theorem assumes a \emph{non-exploring} learner; it shows that exploration is \emph{necessary} for cheap self-evaluation, not that every agent fails. Whether a well-designed joint agent $(\mathcal{L},\mathcal{E})$ that explores can keep $\Gamma_T$ and $\Rex_T$ both sublinear in a genuine big world is the natural next question, and we leave it open (Section~\ref{sec:limits-disc}). What the result does establish is the shape of the trade-off: cheap, trustworthy self-evaluation requires the learner to explore and the examiner to have the capacity to represent what exploration reveals, which is exactly the budget tension $K = K_L + K_E$.

\section{Step 4: Toward Practical Use}
\label{sec:practical}

Although our results are theoretical, the construction is directly estimable, which is part of its appeal as an evaluation tool. \textbf{[heuristic]} A practical examiner can estimate the aspiration $\Vhi$ as an optimistic value, for instance the upper quantile (or maximum head) of an ensemble of value functions or a UCB-style $Q$-estimate, and the guarantee $\Vlow$ as a pessimistic value, the lower quantile of the same ensemble or a conservative critic \citep{lu2021bitbybit,arumugam2024capacity}. The examiner regret $\rho_t$ is then the ensemble's value-uncertainty gap, computable online with no access to the environment's optimum. Three uses follow. First, as a \emph{learning-progress and exploration signal}: acting to reduce $\rho_t$ drives the agent toward states where its aspiration and guarantee disagree, the lineage of compression- and learning-progress drives \citep{schmidhuber2010formal,oudeyer2007intrinsic}, but in value units with a soundness target. Second, as a \emph{model-agnostic comparison metric}: running different RL algorithms on the same continual stream and comparing their $\rho_t$ trajectories estimates which is closer to its own capacity-constrained optimum, without needing $V^{*}$. Third, soundness becomes an empirically checkable property, the calibration of the ensemble, so a practitioner can audit whether a low $\rho_t$ is trustworthy rather than the empty report of Proposition~\ref{prop:nothing}. We do not run these experiments here; we note the path because it is what makes the definition usable rather than only descriptive.

\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limits-disc}

We list the main limitations explicitly. \textbf{(L1) The content is in soundness, not in $\rho_t$.} Proposition~\ref{prop:nothing} is honest about this: the intrinsic object certifies nothing alone, and soundness (S1)--(S2) is extrinsic, requiring the examiner to relate its outputs to value, e.g.\ via a posterior over a model class. The ``model-free'' part of the proposal is only the self-report; the certificate is not free. \textbf{(L2) The bridge is a decomposition, not a new rate} (Theorem~\ref{thm:bridge} is the OFU optimism gap, relabeled); its value is conceptual. \textbf{(L3) The novel regime is unproven.} Our theorems with $\delta_t \to 0$ are shown only in the full-capacity stationary bandit; soundness of a capacity-bounded examiner under genuine non-stationarity, and the under-capacity case $\Dstruct_t > 0$, are not established here. \textbf{[GAP]} \textbf{(L4) Consistency may be unattainable} in the adversarial big world that motivates the work: (S3) $\Gamma_T = o(T)$ asks the examiner to track a moving comparator with sublinear cumulative slack, which may be impossible without further structure. \textbf{(L5) No formal learner--examiner coupling.} $\mathcal{L}$ and $\mathcal{E}$ are separate maps with no feedback channel; the ``explore to escape Theorem~\ref{thm:cost}'' resolution and the allocation $K_E^{*}$ are conceptual, with no mechanism in the present formalism. \textbf{(L6) Theorem~\ref{thm:cost} is conditional on a non-exploring learner} and the impossibility for a good joint agent is open. \textbf{(L7) The capacity model is coarse}, counting representable policies ($|\Theta_L| \le 2^{K_L}$) and ignoring computation. \textbf{(L8) Novelty over rate-distortion satisficing} \citep{arumugam2024capacity} is the explicit examiner module, the certify-nothing thesis (Proposition~\ref{prop:nothing}), and the impossibility framing, rather than a new bound.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\textbf{Foundations and bounded rationality.} \citet{abel2023definition,abel2024dogmas,abel2020bounded} define and develop CRL and the bounded-rationality view of agents; bounded optimality \citep{russell1995bounded}, computational rationality \citep{lewis2014computational}, and resource-rational analysis \citep{lieder2020resource} motivate measuring a bounded agent against its own resources, our comparator $f^{K_L}$. \textbf{Big-world hypothesis.} \citet{javed2025bigworld,lewandowski2025bigger}. \textbf{Learning progress.} Compression- and learning-progress signals \citep{schmidhuber2010formal,oudeyer2007intrinsic} are self-assessed drives; $\rho_t$ is a value-unit relative with a soundness target. \textbf{Capacity-bounded RL.} \citet{lu2021bitbybit} cast capacity in bits; \citet{arumugam2024capacity} build $K_E$-bounded rate-distortion posteriors with within-capacity targets analogous to $f^{K_L}$, the closest prior work. \textbf{Bayes-adaptive / universal RL.} \citet{guez2012bamcp,lidayan2025bampf,hutter2005aixi}. \textbf{Regret, calibration, certification.} OFU \citep{jaksch2010ucrl2,auer2002ucb}, PAC-MDP value bounds \citep{strehl2009pacmdp}, calibration \citep{foster1998calibration}, KWIK \citep{li2011kwik}, non-stationary regret \citep{wei2021master}, hindsight rationality \citep{morrill2021hindsight}. Across these the evaluator is implicit or fused with the policy; we make it an explicit, capacity-bounded module and ask what its self-report is worth.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

We proposed examiner regret as a capacity-aware, agent-internal measure of learning, and were deliberate about what it does and does not deliver. As a self-report it is free and certifies nothing (Proposition~\ref{prop:nothing}); under a soundness condition it certifies the regret a bounded agent can actually reduce (Theorem~\ref{thm:bridge}); and that soundness is not free in a big world, requiring exploration and examiner capacity (Theorem~\ref{thm:cost}). The framework reframes evaluation in CRL as a budgeting problem, $K = K_L + K_E$, between acting and judging, and it is directly estimable as a learning-progress and comparison signal (Section~\ref{sec:practical}). We claim no new algorithm or rate; the contribution is the object, the honest account of its price, and the questions it makes precise, foremost the characterization of a good joint $(\mathcal{L},\mathcal{E})$ and of the optimal split $K_E^{*}$.
